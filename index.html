Privacy for ArrStack
on iOS and Android

ArrStack is a client app for connecting to the media and download services you choose to run. ArrStack does not operate a backend that receives your server activity, media library data, or connection details.

What ArrStack Does Not Collect

ArrStack has no backend that collects your data. The app stores data locally to function, but none of it is sent to ArrStack.

  • No analytics, behavioral tracking, or advertising identifiers
  • No crash-reporting or telemetry operated by ArrStack
  • No media library contents or server connection details sent to ArrStack

What the App Stores Locally

ArrStack stores information on your device so it can connect to the services you configure.

  • Server names, URLs, ports, API keys, and connection credentials
  • Session data such as authentication cookies
  • App settings, preferences, and feature state

Exported backup files may also contain this data and are under your control.

Network Requests

Requests go directly from your device to the servers you configure (Sonarr, Radarr, Bazarr, Tautulli, qBittorrent, SABnzbd, NZBGet, Overseerr, Uptime Kuma, etc.). ArrStack does not proxy requests through its own servers.

Purchases are handled by Apple or Google on their respective platforms.

Android

Configuration is stored locally on-device. The main configuration and session stores are excluded from Android cloud backup and device-transfer rules. ArrStack does not provide cloud backup.

iOS

Configuration is stored locally on-device and may be included in iCloud backups if enabled on your device.

Retention and Deletion

Your data stays on your device. To remove it: delete servers or clear app data, uninstall the app, and delete any exported backup files.

Third-Party Services

The services you connect to are controlled by their operators, not ArrStack. Their privacy and data handling practices are their own.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated. The latest version is indicated by the "Last Updated" date above.
